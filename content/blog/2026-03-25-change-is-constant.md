## Some cool architectural research

[Neural Thickets](https://thickets.mit.edu/index.html) suggest there are dense pockets of experts around your pretrained weight location and they're pretty easy to find.

What this might mean: Terence mentioned labs liking astronomers because they look for significance with little data. This feels smiliar - looking for experts or planets. This could be key to making small language models punch above their weight class.

Deepseek showing again it's the real Open AI with [Engrams](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xUlX6jvwVfM). Adding a module to the transformer to detect multi-token patterns like "piece of cake" or "Alexander the Great" improves performance. There more to it to make it efficient and they not afraid to share.

[Attention Residuals](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.15031) adds attention to residual connections so layers can be more flexible in leveraging earlier representations rather than only looking at what it receives from the previous layer. [This video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LSHTkbnmzy4) explains it way better.

Lots to reproduce and try!
